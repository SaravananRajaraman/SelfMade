Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 5, 2026

Overview

SelfMade is a personal habit and health tracking app. Your data is stored locally on your device by default. Google account connection is optional and only used to sync your data to your own Google Sheet.

Data We Collect

When you use SelfMade without connecting Google:

  • All habit, health, and tracking data is stored only in your browser's local storage (IndexedDB / localStorage).
  • Nothing is sent to any server.

When you choose to connect your Google account:

  • Your Google email address — used only to display which account is connected.
  • An OAuth access token — used only to write data to the Google Sheet you specify. It is stored locally in your browser and never sent to us.

How We Use Your Data

  • Your tracking data is used solely to display your progress within the app.
  • Your Google credentials are used solely to sync data to your own Google Sheet on demand.
  • We do not analyse, profile, or process your data in any way.

Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, or transfer your data to any third party. The only external service involved is Google Sheets (controlled entirely by you) when you explicitly trigger a sync.

Google API Usage

SelfMade uses the Google Identity Services (OAuth 2.0) to request an access token. The following Google API scopes are requested:

  • spreadsheets — to write your habit data to a Google Sheet you own.
  • userinfo.email — to display your connected email address.

SelfMade's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Data Retention & Deletion

All data is stored in your browser. You can delete all app data at any time by clearing your browser's site data for this page, or by using the disconnect option in the Sync tab. Revoking access via your Google Account settings removes this app's access to your Google account immediately.

Children's Privacy

SelfMade is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.
